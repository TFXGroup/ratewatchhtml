The end of the year is one of the busiest times around the world for sending money overseas. Whether you are celebrating Christmas, Diwali, Hanukkah, the New Year or any other seasonal tradition, it is a time when many people send gifts, support family abroad or make international payments before the holidays begin.

With banks working reduced hours, public holidays stacking up and transfer volumes increasing, timing becomes even more important. The earlier you prepare, the smoother your transfer will be.

Here is your simple, inclusive guide to sending money abroad during the global holiday season with Travel FX.

Why the Holiday Season Creates Transfer Delays

November through January brings a peak in global celebrations. Many countries have:

  • Public holidays

  • Shorter banking hours

  • Higher transfer volumes

  • Slower local processing

This combination can occasionally extend transfer times, so planning ahead is always beneficial.

How Long Do International Transfers Take?

Transfer times typically range from one to three working days, depending on the destination and receiving bank. Factors that can influence this include:

  • The currency you are sending

  • Banking hours in the receiving country

  • Local and international public holidays

  • The time of day you initiate your transfer

  • Accuracy of the recipients details

Transfers may take slightly longer during the holiday period, so earlier is always better.

Recommended Seasonal Cut Off Dates

Since every country and bank operates differently, we cannot guarantee specific arrival times. As a general rule:

Aim to send your transfer by mid December
to give it the best chance of arriving before the main holiday closures.

If you are sending money for events that fall earlier, such as Diwali, send your transfer at least a few working days in advance.

Transfers sent later in December are still possible, but allowing extra time helps avoid unnecessary stress.

Tips to Ensure Your Transfer Arrives in Time

A few simple steps can help keep things running smoothly:

  • Double check the recipients bank details

  • Make sure you have your ID or verification ready

  • Send transfers earlier in the working day

  • Avoid weekends and local holidays

  • Let your recipient know the transfer is on the way

Small checks can prevent big delays.

How Travel FX Supports You During Holiday Transfers

Travel FX makes sending money abroad at this time of year simple, safe and transparent. When you request a transfer, you benefit from:

  • Clear rates and no hidden fees

  • Fast and reliable processing

  • Strong security checks to protect your funds

  • Helpful support when you need it

  • Trusted UK regulatory oversight through UKFX Holdings Ltd

We are here to help your money reach its destination with confidence.

Send Your Seasonal Transfer in Good Time

Whether you are sending a gift, supporting family overseas or settling end of year costs, planning ahead ensures your transfer arrives when it matters most.

Why People Worry About International Transfers

If you have never sent money abroad before, it is normal to feel cautious. The biggest concerns usually include:

  • “What if my money disappears?”

  • “What if I choose the wrong provider?”

These are all completely valid questions. The international payments world can feel confusing, but the reality is that reputable providers operate under strict regulations designed to keep your funds and personal information secure.

How Regulated Providers Keep Your Money Safe

In the UK, international money transfer companies must follow strong rules to protect customers. Key safeguards include:

FCA Regulation

Trusted providers are regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). This means they must meet strict standards on transparency, compliance and customer protection.

Safeguarding of Client Funds

Your money must be held in segregated, safeguarded accounts that are completely separate from the companys operational funds. This protects your transfer in the unlikely event that a firm experiences financial difficulty.

Anti Fraud and Anti Money Laundering Controls

Regulated providers are required to use advanced identity checks, fraud monitoring and compliance reviews to ensure transfers are legitimate and secure.

Robust Banking Partners

Reputable providers only work with well established, tier one banking institutions for sending and receiving funds.

In short, a legitimate, regulated transfer company is designed to protect you.

At Travel FX, we believe sending money abroad should be simple, transparent, and great value without losing pounds in the process.

The hidden cost of convenience

Banks and neo-banks make sending money overseas convenient, but convenience often comes at a price.

When you transfer money through them, you usually pay in two ways:

  1. Uncompetitive exchange rates Many banks and even app-based providers apply a hidden markup of 25% on top of the true market rate.
  2. Transfer or service fees High-street banks often charge £20£40 per transaction, and some neo-banks add fees for instant or weekend transfers.

For example: Sending £5,000 abroad with a 3% margin means you lose around £150 immediately, before any transfer charges are added.

Repeat that a few times a year for property payments, family support, or travel expenses, and you could be out hundreds of pounds in unnecessary costs.

Even online banks arent always transparent

Neo-banks and online platforms have changed the way we move money, but not always how much it costs.

Many promote “low fees” and “real rates”, yet:

  • They may still use their own internal pricing rather than the pure market rate.
  • They often apply mark-ups that arent obvious until you check the total received.
  • Fees can vary by currency, amount, or speed of delivery.

So while theyre quicker and more user-friendly than old-school banks, they dont always offer the best overall value.

What is the market rate?

The market rate, also known as the interbank rate, is the real exchange rate used by banks and major financial institutions when trading currencies between themselves. Its the purest, most accurate reflection of a currencys value, sitting exactly between the buy and sell prices.

However, this rate isnt available to the general public. Every retail bank, money app, or transfer company adds a small margin to make a profit and cover operational costs. Thats why youll never get the full interbank rate on a personal transfer, but you can get closer to it with the right provider.
